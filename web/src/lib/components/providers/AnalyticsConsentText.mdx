### INFORMED CONSENT TO PARTICIPATE IN A RESEARCH PROJECT:

_“Impact of Interactive Educational Games on Student Engagement and Learning Outcomes in Quantum Computing Education”_

#### INTRODUCTION

This form asks for your agreement to participate in a research project on the impact of interactive education on quantum computing learning. Your participation involves the anonymous collection of data related to pages viewed, view duration, activities interacted with, interaction duration, and response correctness. It is expected that your participation is for the duration of use of the site. If you are interested in participating, please review the following information.

#### PURPOSE OF THE STUDY AND PROPOSED BENEFITS

- The purpose of the study is to study the effects of interactive education on learning outcomes relating to quantum computing in an effort to improve quantum computing education.
- Potential benefits associated with the study include a better understanding of the emerging field of quantum computing.

#### YOUR PARTICIPATION

- If you agree to participate, you will be asked to participate with as much or as little content as you choose. Potential content includes: text-based lessons, pre- and post-lesson quizzes, figures and visual demonstrations, and short lesson-driven games.
- Your participation will take as long as you choose to interact with the site.

#### PROTECTIONS AND POTENTIAL RISKS

- Please be aware that you are not required to participate in this research, refusal to participate will not involve any penalty or loss of benefits to which you are otherwise entitled, and you may discontinue your participation at any time. If you decide to withdraw your participation, you must visit the About page of quanta.akingstudio.com and unselect the “Allow Collection of Usage Analytics for Research” You may omit responses to any questions you choose not to answer. The researcher may terminate your participation at any time for the following reasons: the creation of excess analytics due to intentional misuse.
- There are minimal risks anticipated with your participation in this study.
- Your confidentiality will be protected by a randomly generated identification number attached only to your single device, within the session storage of your browser. No identifiable data related to a person’s identity will be collected.
