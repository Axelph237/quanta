# Quantum Circuitry
_**A quantum circuit is a sequence of operations applied to qubits.**_ You could think of a quantum circuit as the code that runs on a quantum computer. 

Let's define some key vocabulary. The *width* of a circuit is the number of qubits a circuit contains. The *depth* of a circuit is the number of layers of gates in a circuit. Both of these metrics can help us to understand how expensive a circuit could be to run!
## Circuit Diagrams
A quantum circuit diagram is typically illustrated with horizontal lines representing qubits on the system. Operations (or *gates*) are shown as boxes from left to right along these horizontal lines, representing the order of operations in the circuit. Here is an example circuit diagram:
<Callout title="Example Circuit" icon={<icons.SchrodingersCat className="icon" />}>
<div className="flex items-center justify-center">
<CircuitCanvas numQubits={2} numColumns={2} initialGates={[

    ["x", [1]]

</div>
</Callout>
Here, $q_0$ is undergoing a <span className="define-cx-gate">Controlled-NOT</span> gate with $q_1$ as its target at time-step 0. It then undergoes a <span className="define-x-gate">Pauli-X</span> (also called NOT) gate at time-step 1. We will get more into what these specific gates actually mean in a later lesson. 

The time-steps are not explicitly labeled as it is typically unimportant for the user to understand them beyond the order of operations. But for now, this diagram will serve as a trivial example.
