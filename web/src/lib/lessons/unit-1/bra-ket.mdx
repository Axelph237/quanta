# Bra-Ket Notation
Often, you will see the quantum wave equation $\Psi$ often shown surrounded by these $|...\rangle$ or these $\langle ... |$ symbols. So what are these? The aptly named "bra" $\langle ...|$ and "ket" $|...\rangle$ in short represent vectors!


The **ket** $|...\rangle$ essentially represents a <span className="define-vector">vector</span> $\alpha$ such that $a=|a\rangle$. Essentially, these just represent the vector itself. For instance, if $\Psi = \frac1{\sqrt2} (|0\rangle + |1\rangle)$, then $|\Psi\rangle = \frac1{\sqrt2} \begin{bmatrix}1\\1\end{bmatrix}$ 

The **bra** $\langle ...|$ (although largely unused in our lessons) represents the <span className="define-hconjugate">Hermitian conjugate</span> of our vector $a$ such that $a^\dagger=\langle a|$. Don't worry too much about these :) they aren't super necessary for most of the lessons. In fewer words, the **bra** represents the row vector of $\alpha$. Using the above example, $\langle\Psi| = \frac1{\sqrt2} \begin{bmatrix}1&1\end{bmatrix}$

The differences between row and column vectors is unimportant for the remainder of Quanta, so feel free to not think too deeply about it!

## In Quantum Computing
For our purposes in quantum computing, bras and kets are used to represent the possible states of a system (usually expressed as a <span className="define-binary-string">binary string</span>). Most of the time, we just use kets in our equations. Here's an example state with 4 <span className="define-qbit">qubits</span>:

$$
\Psi=|0010\rangle
$$
where $|\Psi_\alpha\rangle|\Psi_\beta\rangle=|\alpha\beta\rangle$. So, in the above example (from left to right), $q_0 = 0, q_1 = 0, q_2 = 1$ and $q_3 = 0$.
